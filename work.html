Changi General Hospital

Singapore | 2019

In 2019, I was blessed with the opportunity to participate as an international intern in the cardiology department at Changi General Hospital in Singapore. During my time at this hospital, I was under the tutelage of 4 senior consultant cardiologists and performed daily tasks, including making ward rounds, interacting with patients, and observing minimally invasive heart surgeries.

This experience allowed me to develop highly effective problem-solving skills through assessing and analyzing unique cardiovascular issues, as well as strengthen existing communication skills by interacting with over 150 patients a week in daily ward rounds and cardiac clinics (Private and general cardiac clinics, heart device clinic, heart failure clinic and police force clinic).

Furthermore, I honed my attention to detail skills through observation of surgical procedures in the operating room, such as device implantation (Pacemakers and defibrillators), atrial fibrillation ablation, supraventricular tachycardia (SVT) ablation, etc.

My teamwork abilities were once again refined through collaboration with National University of Singapore medical students to review case studies.

Finally, I was able to perform bi-weekly observational attachments at the National Heart Center of Singapore to view electrophysiology procedures of the heart.
